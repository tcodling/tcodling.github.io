Why I am at Dev Academy and how I plan to tackle it

12 February 2020

I am not totally sure what my long-term career pathway is! Right now I am very much focused on improving my skills in tech and web development as much as possible in order to open up as many opportunities as I can in the future. That’s pretty much why I joined Dev Academy! I really just want to upskill and learn without having to go through the 3-4 year learning process that is University.

I am really excited to get some more experience working on group projects with other people. My learning journey so far has been very individual so I am so keen to work in a setting of giving and receiving advice while we work through our problems. I also really want to see my project planning skills increase as that has always been a weakness of mine. I generally just jump into a project without any kind of structure towards finding my solution. I think strategies and skills in this area will make my productivity increase hugely as well as increase the quality of my projects when finished.

In order to make sure I work as hard and efficiently as possible, I found myself a single room in Wellington very close to campus. I thought about staying with friends to maybe save money but I thought having my own space where I can focus without distractions is going to be incredibly helpful.

I am very aware that this course is going to be quite a challenge for me in terms of the workload, the content and the teamwork aspect. However I am so motivated and I really believe I can do it. Communication between myself and the facilitators is going to be crucial. They are going to be a great tool for keeping me on track with the details of the course. However this is my own learning journey that I am responsible for. I really want to be taught how to find answers and solve problems as opposed to being taught answers and solutions.

I already know a few handy websites I can go for help but the Dev Academy has set up some great resources for us. The slack channels, particularly the code help desk, sound incredibly helpful. I think having access to other people who have previously or are currently going through the bootcamp is going to be super beneficial for everyone involved. My foundations coordinator Joseph has been a great source of help when I have questions about the course! The facilitation team has been great so far and I don’t really expect much from them other to keep me in the loop about the course, correct me when I am making huge mistakes without noticing and to answer my really hard questions or to push me in the right direction towards getting those answers.

I don't have much scheduled for during my course, however I have been doing a few shifts working as a cleaner at the stadium during the beginning of foundations which has made my days slightly scattered sometimes. This won’t be a problem at all when I am in Wellington and I really get stuck into the boot camp.
